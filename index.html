Operational definition of 'particles'

"There are quantum states and there are particle detectors. Quantum field theory enables us to predict probabilistically how a particular detector will respond to that state. That is all."

- Paul Davies [1]

 

[1] P. C. W. Davies, Particles Do Not Exist in Quantum Theory of Gravity, 1984.

What would a relativistic detector see?

Can model it as a delocalised Klein-Gordon field

\[ \phi_j(x) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \int \frac{d^3\mathbf{p}}{\sqrt{2E_j(\mathbf{p})}} \left( a_j(\mathbf{p}) e^{-ip \cdot x} + a^\dagger_j(\mathbf{p}) e^{ip \cdot x} \right) \]

or as a localised Newton-Wigner field [2]

\[ \varphi_j(x) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \int d^3\mathbf{p} \, a_j(\mathbf{p}) e^{-ip \cdot x} \]
 

[2] S. A. Fulling, Aspects of Quantum Field Theory in Curved Spacetime, 1989.

Klein-Gordon detector?

Philips position state [3] given by

\[ \phi_j^\dagger(x) | 0 \rangle = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \int \frac{d^3\mathbf{p}}{\sqrt{2E_j(\mathbf{p})}} e^{ip \cdot x} | \mathbf{p}, j \rangle \]

Lorentz covariant and causal! But there are some problems...

  • Position states non-orthogonal
  • Spectrum of Hamiltonian is unbounded
  • Klein-Gordon inner product is indefinite
 

[3] T. O. Philips, Phys. Rev. 136, B893 (1964).

Newton-Wigner detector?

Newton-Wigner position state [4] given by

\[ \varphi_j^\dagger(x) | 0 \rangle = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \int d^3\mathbf{p} \, e^{ip \cdot x} | \mathbf{p}, j \rangle \, . \]

Not Lorentz covariant... but problems solved!

  • Position states are orthogonal
  • Spectrum of Hamiltonian is bounded below
  • Inner product is positive definite
 

[4] T. D. Newton and E. P. Wigner, Rev. Mod. Phys. 21, 400 (1949).

Dual representations of the Poincaré group

Klein-Gordon detector solves hyperbolic wave equation

\[ (\Box + m^2) \phi(x) = 0 \, . \]
 

Newton-Wigner detector solves pseudodifferential wave equation

\[ i \partial_t \varphi_j(x) = \sqrt{-\nabla^2 + m_j^2} \, \varphi_j(x) \, , \]

...what does a particle detector detect?

Transition rate is dependent on localisation scheme! [5]

  • Klein-Gordon:
    • \[ \dot{P}^{\mathrm{(KG)}}[\psi_i](\mathbf{p}) \propto \int d^3\mathbf{p} \, |\psi_i(\mathbf{p}; \mathbf{p}_0)|^2 \, \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m_e^2}} \, . \]
  • Newton-Wigner:
    • \[ \dot{P}^{\mathrm{(NW)}}[\psi_i](\mathbf{p}) \propto \int d^3\mathbf{p} \, |\psi_i(\mathbf{p}; \mathbf{p}_0)|^2 \, \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m_e^2} \, . \]
 

[5] E. P. G. Gale and M. Zych, Phys. Rev. D 107, 056023 (2023).

Summary

  • Dual representations of the Poincaré group with differing spectrum
    •  
  • Newton-Wigner representation:
    • localised
    • Hermitian $\hat{x}$
    • positive-definite inner product
    •  
  • Klein-Gordon representation:
    • causal
    • non-Hermitian $\hat{x}$
    • indefinite inner product
